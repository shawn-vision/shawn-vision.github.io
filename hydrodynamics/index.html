What does ‚ÄúHydrodynamics‚Äù mean in our group?

In our work, hydrodynamics is a bridge between turbulence physics and real-world transport: how momentum, energy, particles, and scalars move through rivers, wetlands, and coastal systems.

We focus on:

  • deriving transport laws from turbulence budgets and spectra
  • quantifying how roughness and vegetation reshape flow structure and mixing
  • predicting suspension, settling, scour, and deposition across regimes
  • building models that remain interpretable, scalable, and validated

Rather than relying only on empirical correlations, we aim to connect observations to mechanisms‚Äîso models remain reliable under nonstationary forcing and climate-driven extremes.
