Why wall friction is a fundamental turbulence problem

A central challenge in fluid mechanics is explaining energy dissipation in turbulent flows arising from friction at boundaries. This ‚Äúwall friction‚Äù problem directly controls pressure drop and transport efficiency in pipelines and channels‚Äîcritical for moving oil, methane, and water across continents.

Classical practice relies on empirical Nikuradse/Moody diagrams linking friction factor to Reynolds number and relative roughness. Our goal is to replace curve-fitting with a first-principles mechanism grounded in turbulence theory.
