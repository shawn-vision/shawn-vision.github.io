### Why turbulence matters for sediment transport

A long-standing assumption in sediment transport theory is that the
<strong>settling velocity of particles in turbulence</strong> is identical to that in still water.
This simplification underpins classical models such as <strong>Rouse‚Äôs equation</strong>.

<div style="
  background: #F1FAFF;
  border-left: 6px solid #0055A4;
  padding: 14px 18px;
  border-radius: 12px;
  margin: 18px 0;
  color:#0F172A;
">
Our work shows that turbulence fundamentally alters settling dynamics:
even weak fluctuations can <strong>reduce effective settling velocity</strong>
through history and inertia effects.
</div>

Using <strong>perturbation analysis</strong>, we derived one of the first analytical models
for sediment settling in turbulent flows, explicitly accounting for:

- **Basset history force**  
- **Virtual mass effects**  

These mechanisms explain persistent discrepancies between classical theory,
observations, and formulations based on the <strong>turbulent Schmidt number</strong>.

### Turbulence, roughness, and vegetation‚Äìsediment interaction

Sediment transport is strongly modified by <strong>boundary roughness</strong>,
including aquatic vegetation and engineered structures.

<div style="
  background: #F1FAFF;
  border-left: 6px solid #0055A4;
  padding: 14px 18px;
  border-radius: 12px;
  margin: 18px 0;
  color:#0F172A;
">
We link <strong>microscale turbulence physics</strong> to
<strong>field-scale sediment and contaminant dynamics</strong>
in vegetated and rough-bed flows.
</div>

Key contributions include:

- Development of an <strong>asymmetric eddy diffusivity model</strong> for vegetated channels  
- Derivation of a <strong>universal velocity distribution</strong> using asymptotic analysis  
- Numerical <strong>reactive transport modeling</strong> of contaminant uptake by vegetation  
- Quantification of <strong>floating treatment wetland efficiency</strong>, validated against field data  

---

### What this framework enables

- **Physically grounded sediment settling models** in turbulent flows  
- **Unified interpretation of suspension theory**, beyond empirical fits  
- **Improved predictions of sediment and contaminant transport** in vegetated systems  
- **Scalable links from turbulence physics to ecosystem-scale performance**
